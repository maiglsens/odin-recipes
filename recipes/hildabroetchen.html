Hildabrötchen

This is my grandma's amazing Hildabrötchen recipe

Like with my mum's Linzertorte, there is nothing much to say here, except: Try and be amazed ;-)

Ingredients

For the dough
  • 300g wheat flour
  • 150g butter or margarine
  • 120g sugar
  • 1 pck vanillin sugar
  • 1 egg
For the filling and icing
  • raspberry jam
  • 125g powdered sugar
  • 2 tbsp hot water
  • a few drops of a lemon

Steps

  1. Sieve the flour into a bowl then cut the butter into tiny pieces before adding them into the bowl together with the sugar, vanillin sugar and egg.

  2. Mix it all together and knead until you got a smooth and even consistency.

  3. Put the dough into the refridgerator for an hour.

  4. After cooling take the dough and roll it out on a flat surface. Make sure it has an even thikness of about 3mm.

  5. Now take a round or flower shaped cookie cutter (~5cm diameter) and cut out the whole dough. Make sure you have an even number at the end, as they will go in pairs.

  6. Using one half of the cookies you now want to cut out smaller holes of the same shape (round or flower shaped) from their middle.

  7. Put all the cookies (with and without hole) onto a baking tray and bake them for 10-12 minutes at 170°C.

  8. When done baking let them cool off for a bit before spreading the raspberry jam on all the cookies without a hole in the middle.

  9. Now press the remaining cookies (the ones with the holes in the middle) lightly onto the jam. We're nearly done now.

  10. The only thing missing now is the icing. Take the powdered sugar and mix it with the hot water and the lemon. You now should have a gooey sugar icing.

  11. Finally apply the icing onto the Hildabrötchen (the part with the hole in it) and you're done.

  12. I always let the Hildabrötchen sit for a while so the dough can soak in a bit of the jam what makes them even more delicious.

  13. Enjoy! :-)
